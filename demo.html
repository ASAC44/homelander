Slack Agent Builder Challenge 2026 / New Slack Agent

Homelander

Trade intelligence that starts and finishes in Slack.

Homelander turns a shipment question into a sourced decision packet. It gathers missing details, researches trade and logistics signals through MCP-powered tools, calculates landed cost, and returns a concise answer with evidence and a versioned report.

The Idea

Homelander is a Slack-native international trade intelligence agent for teams planning cross-border shipments. A user sends the product, origin, destination, shipment mode, timing, weight, and value in a direct message or mention. Homelander asks for anything missing and turns the conversation into structured shipment data.

It coordinates specialist agents to research routes, ports, customs, tariffs, required documents, freight conditions, weather, policy changes, and supply-chain risk. Deterministic code calculates landed cost and weighted risk. Homelander then replies in the same Slack thread with a concise recommendation, a temporary evidence file, and a permanent versioned PDF report containing sources and assumptions.

Why It Matters

A cross-border shipping decision can require customs databases, port updates, freight signals, weather, policy changes, commodity exposure, and cost models. Teams often collect those pieces manually while the decision window keeps moving.

Homelander brings that work into one familiar conversation. The team gets a clear recommendation while every important claim stays tied to evidence.

Why Slack

Shipment decisions already happen between operations, finance, procurement, and leadership in Slack. Homelander meets the team there, keeps context in the current thread, and returns a compact answer that can be discussed immediately.

No new dashboard is required. The Slack summary and full report come from the same structured model, so the quick answer and the detailed record stay consistent.

Who We Built It For

Homelander is designed for small and mid-sized manufacturers, importers, distributors, and retailers that handle recurring international shipments and coordinate decisions in Slack. These companies need reliable trade research but may not have a large in-house customs, logistics, or trade intelligence function.

Target Slack teams

  • Import, export, and trade operations teams managing active shipments.
  • Supply chain and logistics teams comparing routes, ports, delays, and documents.
  • Procurement teams evaluating shipment feasibility and landed cost for approved products and vendors.
  • Finance teams reviewing duties, taxes, freight assumptions, and total landed cost.
  • Customs and trade compliance teams reviewing classifications, requirements, and risk.

Target Slack users

  • Trade operations managers and import or export coordinators.
  • Logistics coordinators and supply chain analysts.
  • Procurement managers working on approved shipment plans.
  • Landed-cost analysts, finance analysts, and controllers.
  • Customs specialists and trade compliance managers.
  • Heads of supply chain, operations directors, and COOs reviewing the final recommendation.

The daily user is usually a trade operations or logistics lead. Finance, procurement, compliance, and leadership join the same Slack thread as reviewers.

Core Features

  • Slack-native shipment intake in DMs and explicit mention threads.
  • Clarifying questions for missing or ambiguous shipment fields.
  • Public-web retrieval with source URLs, snippets, and search logs.
  • Product profiling with HS candidates, materials, and cost dependencies.
  • Route and port comparison with congestion and wait-time signals.
  • Tariff, customs, and documentation findings with verification warnings.
  • Risk analysis across commodity, freight, port, weather, geopolitical, supplier, and regulatory categories.
  • Deterministic landed-cost calculations kept outside the LLM.
  • Concise Slack summary plus complete versioned HTML and PDF reports.
  • Temporary evidence file with assumptions, sources, excerpts, calculations, and timestamps.
  • Follow-up Q&A against the current thread and completed report context.
  • Human-approved next actions with no autonomous booking, filing, or purchasing.

Trust by Design

Homelander supports a human decision. It does not book freight, file customs entries, make purchases, change routes, or present research as binding legal or tax advice.

Evidence firstSources, excerpts, assumptions, confidence, and retrieval times travel with the analysis.
Numbers stay deterministicLanded cost and weighted risk calculations run in code, outside the language model.
Context stays focusedThe MVP responds to DMs and explicit mentions and reads only the originating Slack thread.
People stay in controlExternal or consequential actions require human review and confirmation.
